About Me

Public safety GIS professional learning more about GIS, cartography, and geospatial development.

Public Safety, GIS, and Geography

I am a Kentucky expat who has lived and worked across several states, including Wisconsin and Georgia. My professional background is rooted in public safety at the local, state and federal levels. I am now applying that experience in GIS, and public safety mapping as the public safety GIS analyst for Chatham County GA.

I have spent more than ten years with the Department of Justice across multiple agencies and duty stations. That background has given me experience with research, operations, records, database query and analysis, CJIS policy, interagency coordination, and mission-focused problem solving.

Presently I am in pursuit of a Master of Science degree in GIS Development through the University of Wisconsin-Madison while building projects focused on web GIS, cartography, and public safety applications.

Outside of work, I enjoy spending time with my wife and daughter, reading, playing bass, weightlifting, working on my house and training Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu.
